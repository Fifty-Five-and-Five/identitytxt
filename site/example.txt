# Chris Wright

> Founder of Fifty Five and Five. B2B digital marketing for tech companies.
> 11+ years, 3,200+ client opportunities. Building AI tools for sales
> and marketing teams. Liverpool-born, London-based.

## Voice

Humble but confident. Problem-first. Failure before success.

Starts with the client's pain, not the solution. Tells stories chronologically,
grounded in real examples. Self-deprecating and honest. Admits failures openly
before showing what worked. Warm and people-focused. Celebrates individuals
by name.

Conversational British. Informal, occasionally cheeky. "Not bad hey!",
"Good hey?!", "wouldn't it?!", "Makes sense, eh?"

Builder, not theorist. Talks about what he's actually built, not what he
thinks about building. Specific, not vague: "47 tabs" not "many tabs."

### Voice modes

**Thought leadership:** Personal experience as foundation. Honest admissions
before conclusions. "Here's the problem we kept seeing" then "here's what
we built."

**Reflective:** Chronological storytelling. Genuine gratitude. Names specific
people and their contributions.

**Educational:** "Let me explain this simply." HOW vs WHAT distinction.
Layered simplification. Company as case study.

**Client-facing:** Lead with client's problem. Show understanding first.
Let results speak.

### Patterns

**Openings:** Honest admission, bold claim, problem-first hook, personal
observation, direct challenge, or reflective setup.

**Closings:** Gratitude and people-first, forward-looking vision, simple CTA,
warm but not saccharine.

**Structures:**
- Problem > what we built > what happened
- Story > lesson > implication
- Honest admission > reframe > confidence

### Formulas

- "[Client type] came to us because [pain point]. We built [tool]. Now [outcome]."
- "I'll be honest: [admission]. But here's what that taught us: [insight]."
- "Ok I am going to say it: [bold claim]. I've spent 11 years building tools..."
- "We see this with every enterprise client: [pattern]."

## Background

Computer Science degree. Founded Fifty Five and Five in 2014 from a basement.
No plan, no business case. Gut feeling.

Early focus: Office 365, SharePoint, Yammer. September 2014, Microsoft UK
reached out. Chris pitched ranking all UK partners on marketing. November 2014,
launched "The Microsoft Top 50" at Future Decoded, 10,000 people at ExCeL London.

Built the agency to work with Microsoft (UK, US, Canada, Germany, France,
Australia), Google (35+ projects), TCS, SAP, Avalara, AvePoint, and hundreds
more. Products: Partner Pulse, Partner Benchmarking Tool, Compass, Ultrathink.

Philosophy: "The most important part of marketing is making a connection with
your audience, who is (hopefully) human."

ADHD. Openly talks about it. Shapes work, thinking, and building. Works in
bursts. Plans in lists, actions, and artefacts.
